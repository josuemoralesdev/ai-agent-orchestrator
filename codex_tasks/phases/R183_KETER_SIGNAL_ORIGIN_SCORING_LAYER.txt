KETER SCORE DIMENSIONS:
Each origin should be scored 0-100 using these dimensions when data exists:

1. detector_availability_score
- DETECTOR_AVAILABLE: high
- INFERRED_FROM_EXISTING_FIELDS: medium/high
- REGISTRY_ONLY: low until detector exists
- UNKNOWN: low

2. tagged_data_score
- more tagged records means stronger evidence base
- no tagged records = weak

3. lane_coverage_score
- origin appears across multiple relevant lanes
- or appears strongly on current lead lane

4. freshness_score
- recent tagged examples / fresh flow if available
- stale-only should not dominate

5. historical_outcome_score
- paper outcome count, win rate, avg PnL, total PnL if mappable
- null data should not be hallucinated

6. reversal_context_score
- reversal/rejection origins are relevant for current strategy
- continuation origins are useful but must be separated

7. conflict_penalty
- registry-only origins should be penalized
- unknown/unclassified should be penalized
- detector gaps should be explicit

Suggested score bands:
- 0-24: registry / unknown / not actionable
- 25-49: needs detector or more data
- 50-69: paper tracking candidate
- 70-84: strong paper origin candidate
- 85-100: high-priority origin for next matrix work

IMPORTANT:
- Do not treat Three Black Crows as detected yet.
- Do not give registry-only origins high score just because the operator likes them.
- Three Black Crows can receive high detector-priority, not high trading-readiness.
- Hammer/wick and golden-pocket origins can score higher because existing fields support them.
- No origin can become live-authorized in R183.
